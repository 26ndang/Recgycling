Recycling

BY: NATALIE DANG

What is recycling?

Recycling is the process of breaking down different materials like plastic, paper, glass, and metals to reuse them. This helps save our resources. Most things that people recycle are made into new things and used over and over again, as bottles, cans, paper, and more. Recycling is good for your community and the environment.

Why is Recycling Important?

Things that are not recyclable end up in the landfill. This is bad because trash in landfills just sits there, letting off methane gas while they very slowly break down. Methane gas makes the Greenhouse Gas layer in the atmosphere thicker and our planet hotter, and the other harmful gases it lets off harms the environment and the toxins pollute the air that we breathe and the water that we drink. Lots of things we throw away to go in the landfill can be recycled and saved from being wasted and turned into methane. The pollutants will be reduced if people recycle more. Recycling vs. Landfill

Recycling breaks down materials to use again while items in the landfill sit there and turn into harmful methane gases. Recycling is better for the environment because it saves our natural resources and reduces the amount of greenhouse gases in our air.
